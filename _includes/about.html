About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Applied Statistics at the University of Guelph and I am in my third year of doctoral studies. I passed my qualifying exam in March, 2018 and I am in the process of completing the research component of my thesis. My Ph.D. advisors are Dr. Peter Kim and Dr. Tony Desmond. I am part of the bioinformatics research group at which includes Dr. Zeny Feng.

My research interests are in various classes of statistical models over higher-dimensional surfaces with applications to data in the sciences. I have examined the relationship between the parameters of the generalized Kent distribution and the exponential model with a generalized tesseral harmonic basis; I have developed the Beran LASSO for data over Sp-1 using a Watson kernel density estimator and developed a test for model selection for directional data in higher dimensions. I am currently running simulations and applying the statistical framework developed for real data.

My Erdös number is 4.
