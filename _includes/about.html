About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Applied Statistics at the University of Guelph. I passed my candidacy exam in March, 2018.

My research interests are in various classes of statistical models over higher-dimensional surfaces. I have examined the relationship between the parameters of the generalized Kent distribution and the exponential model with a generalized tesseral harmonic basis, I have developed the Beran LASSO for data over Sp-l using a Watson kernel density esimator, and developed a test for model selection for directional data in higher dimensions. I am currently running simulations and examining applications of the framework developed for real data.

My Ph.D. supervisors are Dr. Peter Kim and Dr. Tony Desmond.
